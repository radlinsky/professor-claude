## Big ideas (backward design)

What this source wants a reader to be able to DO after working through it: [@chan2021probabilitydatascience]

- Construct a probability model from axioms (sample space, events, probability law) and apply Bayes' theorem to update beliefs given data
- Characterize a random variable by its PMF/PDF, CDF, expectation, and variance, and identify which named distribution (Bernoulli, binomial, Poisson, Gaussian, exponential) fits a real-world scenario
- Derive properties of joint, marginal, and conditional distributions, compute covariance and correlation, and work with the multivariate Gaussian (including PCA via eigendecomposition)
- Explain why the sample mean converges (law of large numbers) and why its distribution is approximately normal (central limit theorem), and use these to reason about estimator behavior
- Formulate and solve a linear regression problem (OLS, ridge, LASSO), decompose test error into bias and variance, and choose a regularization strategy
- Compute maximum-likelihood and MAP estimates, connect MAP with ridge regression, and analyze estimator properties (bias, consistency)
- Construct confidence intervals (including via bootstrap), execute hypothesis tests (z-test, t-test, Neyman-Pearson), and evaluate a classifier with ROC and precision-recall curves
