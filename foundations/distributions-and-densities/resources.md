# Resources: Distributions and densities

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [StatQuest: The Normal Distribution, Clearly Explained!!!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rzFX5NWojp0)
  (video, ~5 min) — Josh Starmer builds the bell curve from a pile of measurements
  and shows what the center and spread knobs do, in plain English with small examples.
  Watch right after this lesson's "A tiny example first" section.
- 📺 [StatQuest: Histograms, Clearly Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qBigTkBLU6g)
  (video, ~4 min) — the histogram-to-distribution step this lesson opens with: how
  counting values into bars gives you the shape a smooth curve approximates. Watch
  before or alongside "Getting a feel for it".
- 🎮 [Seeing Theory: Probability Distributions](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/probability-distributions/index.html)
  (interactive) — drag sliders to reshape a normal curve's mean and variance and watch
  the bell move in real time; the fastest way to *feel* the two knobs and that the area
  always totals 1. Play with it after "The formal version".
