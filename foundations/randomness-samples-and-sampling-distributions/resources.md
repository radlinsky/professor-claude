# Resources: Randomness, samples & sampling distributions

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [StatQuest: The Central Limit Theorem, Clearly Explained!!!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YAlJCEDH2uY)
  (video, ~8 min) — plain-English walk through where the bell shape of sample means
  comes from, with small simulated examples. Exactly this lesson's "the bell emerges for
  means" observation, animated. Watch after the "Explore it" histograms.
- 📺 [StatQuest StatQuickie: Standard Deviation vs Standard Error](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A82brFpdr9g)
  (video, ~2 min) — a short, targeted clearing-up of the single most common mistake in
  this area (Trap 1): the spread of the *data* vs the spread of the *statistic*. Watch
  right after the lesson's "SE is NOT the spread of the data" section to nail it down.
- 🎮 [Seeing Theory: Frequentist Inference](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/frequentist-inference/index.html)
  (interactive) — drop samples, resample, and watch the sampling distribution of the
  mean build up, then generate many confidence intervals and see how often they cover the
  truth. The "what a confidence interval is trying to do" idea, made draggable. Use after
  the confidence-interval section of the lesson.
