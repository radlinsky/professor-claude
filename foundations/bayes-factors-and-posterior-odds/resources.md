# Resources — Bayes factors and posterior odds

External explanations of Bayes factors and updating beliefs on the odds scale. Use them
after the lesson, to see the "prior odds × Bayes factor = posterior odds" machine in
another voice.

- 📺 [StatQuest: Bayes' Theorem, Clearly Explained!!!!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9wCnvr7Xw4E)
  (video) — Josh Starmer derives Bayes' theorem step by step from a concrete example and
  explains why it is the foundation of Bayesian reasoning. The lesson rewrites that same
  theorem on the odds scale; watch this first if the probability-form version feels shaky,
  then return to see it become a plain multiplication.
- 📖 [Bayes factor (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayes_factor)
  (reference article) — defines the Bayes factor as the ratio of two models' evidences and,
  usefully, gives the strength-interpretation scales (Jeffreys, and Kass & Raftery) — the
  "barely worth mentioning / substantial / strong / decisive" rules of thumb the lesson
  mentions. Skim it for those interpretation tables once you can compute a Bayes factor.
- 🎮 [Seeing Theory: Bayesian Inference (Brown University)](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/bayesian-inference/index.html)
  (interactive) — the "Bayes' Theorem" panel is the lesson's medical-test anchor made
  clickable: set a disease prevalence (your prior) and a test accuracy, then simulate
  patients and watch how a positive result updates belief. Perfect for internalizing why a
  strong test still leaves a rare disease unlikely. Use it right after the "Getting a feel
  for it" section.
