# Resources: Hypothesis tests, p-values & confidence intervals

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [StatQuest — p-values: what they are and how to interpret them](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vemZtEM63GY)
  (video, ~11 min) — Josh Starmer's plain-English walk through what a p-value *is* and,
  crucially, the "it is NOT the probability the null is true" trap this lesson's Trap 1
  warns about. Watch right after the lesson's "The formal version" section.
- 📺 [StatQuest — How to calculate p-values](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JQc3yx0-Q9E)
  (video, ~25 min) — shows p-values emerging from counting extreme outcomes, the same
  "count the null worlds" logic as this lesson's simulation. Watch if you want to see
  the simulate-and-count idea from another angle.
- 📺 [StatQuest — Confidence Intervals, clearly explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TqOeMYtOc1w)
  (video, ~7 min) — builds a confidence interval by bootstrapping, making the "range that
  usually catches the truth" picture concrete. Watch after the lesson's CI section to
  reinforce the CI-excludes-the-null idea.
- 🎮 [Seeing Theory — Frequentist Inference](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/frequentist-inference/index.html)
  (interactive) — the confidence-interval panel lets you set a confidence level and
  sample repeatedly, watching how often the intervals catch the true value. Play with it
  after the lesson to *feel* the coverage idea behind "95%."
