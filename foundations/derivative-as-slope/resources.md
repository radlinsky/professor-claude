# Resources: The derivative, as a slope you can measure

Verified external explanations for this foundation module. Watch/read alongside the
lesson if a second voice helps.

- 📺 **[3Blue1Brown — The paradox of the derivative (Essence of Calculus, Ch. 2)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9vKqVkMQHKk)**
  (video, ~17 min). Builds the derivative visually as the slope you approach by shrinking
  the step — exactly this lesson's "rise over a tiny run", with gorgeous animation. The
  best possible companion to *Getting a feel for it*; watch it if the "why a tiny step?"
  idea hasn't clicked.

- 📺 **[3Blue1Brown — The Essence of Calculus, Ch. 1](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WUvTyaaNkzM)**
  (video, ~17 min). The big-picture opener of the series: where the idea of a slope /
  rate of change sits in calculus as a whole. Optional context — reassuring if calculus
  has always felt like a bag of disconnected rules; you need none of those rules for this
  course, only the slope idea.
