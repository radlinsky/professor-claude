# Resources: Likelihood and log-likelihood

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [StatQuest — In Statistics, Probability is not Likelihood.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pYxNSUDSFH4)
  (video, ~5 min) — the clearest short take on *the flip* this lesson is built around:
  probability fixes the parameter and varies the data; likelihood fixes the data and
  varies the parameter. Watch after the "Getting a feel for it" section to lock in the
  dial-not-die picture.
- 📺 [StatQuest — Maximum Likelihood, clearly explained!!!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XepXtl9YKwc)
  (video, ~6 min) — walks the maximum-likelihood idea end to end: build the likelihood,
  slide the parameter, find the peak. A gentle visual companion to "The formal version."
- 📖 [Wikipedia — Likelihood function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Likelihood_function)
  (reference) — the reference definition of likelihood, log-likelihood, and the
  maximum-likelihood estimate, plus the note that continuous data swaps probabilities
  for densities (this lesson's soft-mentioned next step). Skim the intro and the
  "Log-likelihood" section; the later material on nuisance parameters is beyond this
  module.
