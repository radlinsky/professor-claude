# Resources: Rank and linear independence

All links verified 2026-07-11.

- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — Linear combinations, span, and basis vectors (Essence of Linear Algebra, Ch. 2)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k7RM-ot2NWY)
  (video, ~10 min) — the animated version of this lesson's "directions of
  information" picture: what it means for vectors to span a space, and what
  goes wrong when one vector is a combination of the others. Watch after the
  "Getting a feel for it" section.
- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — Inverse matrices, column space and null space (Essence of Linear Algebra, Ch. 7)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uQhTuRlWMxw)
  (video, ~12 min) — shows geometrically why a singular matrix has no inverse
  (it squashes space and can't be reversed) and introduces column space as the
  set of reachable outputs. Watch after the "Explore it" section; ignore "null
  space" vocabulary for now — the rank/singularity half is what you need.
- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — Nonsquare matrices as transformations between dimensions (Essence of Linear Algebra, Ch. 8)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v8VSDg_WQlA)
  (video, ~5 min) — a short visual on what it means for a matrix to map between
  different-dimensional spaces, with rank as the dimension of the output.
  Watch after the full lesson to connect rank to the geometric picture.
