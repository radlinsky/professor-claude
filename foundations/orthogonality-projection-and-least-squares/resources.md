## Textbook references

- 📚 **Understanding Linear Algebra** (Austin, 2022) —
  [§6.3 Orthogonal projection](https://scholarworks.gvsu.edu/books/26/) covers
  projection onto a line and a subspace; §6.4 applies projection to least squares and
  the normal equations. The geometric picture (residual ⊥ column space) is the thread
  through both sections. Read after the lesson's formal version section to see the same
  ideas in textbook notation.
  [@austin2022understandinglinearalgebra, pp. 371--403]

- 📚 **Applied Linear Algebra** (Boyd & Vandenberghe, 2018) —
  [Chapter 12](https://web.stanford.edu/~boyd/vmls/) derives the normal equations via
  the gradient, then gives a separate algebraic (orthogonality) proof. Chapter 13
  applies the result to data fitting. Read after the lesson if you want the
  calculus-based derivation alongside the geometric one.
  [@boyd2018appliedlinearalgebra, pp. 225--278]
