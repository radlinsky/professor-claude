# Resources — Quadratic forms & the chi-square statistic

Verified external explanations, if you want another angle. (Every link below was checked this
session.)

- 📖 [JMP: The Chi-Square Distribution](https://www.jmp.com/en/statistics-knowledge-portal/chi-square-test/chi-square-distribution)
  (article with figures) — plain-English walk through how the degrees of freedom parameter
  reshapes the distribution, with a picture of three chi-square curves and how to read a
  chi-square table. Read it after the lesson's "why is it chi-square" section to cement the
  df-changes-the-shape idea.
- 📖 [Wikipedia: Chi-squared distribution (intro & definition)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chi-squared_distribution)
  (reference) — states the key fact this lesson leans on: a chi-square with $k$ degrees of
  freedom **is** the sum of squares of $k$ independent standard normals (so $z^2$ is
  $\chi^2_1$). Skim just the lead and "Definitions" sections; the rest is heavier than you need.
