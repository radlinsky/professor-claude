# Resources: Reading a methods section — the conventions

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📖 [Statology — What Are i.i.d. Random Variables?](https://www.statology.org/i-i-d-random-variables/)
  (post, ~5 min read) — the "iid" convention in plain English, built entirely from
  small everyday examples (coin flips, dice, drawing cards *with replacement* = the
  "same jar" idea). No heavy math. Read alongside this lesson's "iid — the promise
  about your data" section to reinforce the two halves (independent; identically
  distributed).
- 📺 [Big-O notation in 5 minutes — the basics](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=__vX2sjlpXU)
  (video, ~5 min) — a short, beginner-level walk-through of big-O as a *growth-rate
  label* ($O(n)$ vs $O(n^2)$ and friends), exactly the reading level this lesson aims
  for. Watch after the "Asymptotics in words" section to see the growth curves move;
  ignore any code-implementation asides — you only need the "how does it scale?" idea.
- 📖 [Cal Newport — How to Read Proofs Faster](https://calnewport.com/how-to-read-proofs-faster-a-summary-of-useful-advice/)
  (blog post, ~10 min read) — practical advice on reading theorem/lemma/proof structure
  strategically: grasp the main *result* first, map how lemmas feed theorems, and don't
  expect to master every proof line. It's pitched at grad students, so skim past the
  research-workflow bits — the core message ("read the boxed statement, trust it, dip
  into the proof only when you need the *why*") is exactly this lesson's trust-and-skip
  reading move.
