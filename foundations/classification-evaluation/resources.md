# Resources: Classification evaluation

All links verified 2026-07-11.

- 📺 [StatQuest: Machine Learning Fundamentals --- The Confusion Matrix](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kdsp6soqA7o)
  (video, Josh Starmer) --- walks through TP, FP, TN, FN with small numeric examples
  and builds the confusion matrix step by step. Watch after the lesson's tiny example
  to see the same idea presented a second way.
- 📺 [StatQuest: Sensitivity and Specificity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vP06aMoz4v8)
  (video, Josh Starmer) --- covers sensitivity and specificity as two complementary
  ways to evaluate a classifier, with the same plain-English style as the lesson.
  Watch after you are comfortable with the confusion matrix.
- 📺 [StatQuest: ROC and AUC, Clearly Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4jRBRDbJemM)
  (video, Josh Starmer) --- shows how to build an ROC curve from multiple confusion
  matrices and explains AUC as a single summary number. The best companion to the
  lesson's Explore section.
- 🎮 [Interactive ROC Curve Animation](https://www.interactive-ml.com/roc-curve.html)
  (interactive, free, no signup) --- lets you adjust the classification threshold and
  watch the ROC curve build point by point. Try "Excellent," "Good," "Poor," and
  "Random" classifier quality settings to see how data separability affects the
  curve's shape and AUC.
- 📚 [An Introduction to Statistical Learning, Chapter 4](https://www.statlearning.com)
  (book, free PDF) --- Section 4.4.2 is the source of this module's confusion-matrix,
  sensitivity/specificity, ROC, and threshold-trade-off content, including the
  credit-card Default dataset example this practice set borrows numbers from. Read it
  after the lesson for the full worked derivation and more worked examples.
