# Resources: Matrices, and what it means to multiply them

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — Linear transformations and matrices (Essence of Linear Algebra, Ch. 3)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kYB8IZa5AuE)
  (video, ~11 min) — the single best animation of this lesson's core picture: a
  matrix as a *machine that transforms a vector into another vector*. Watch after
  the "Getting a feel for it" section to see the box-eats-a-vector idea move.
- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — Matrix multiplication as composition (Essence of Linear Algebra, Ch. 4)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XkY2DOUCWMU)
  (video, ~10 min) — why $AB \neq BA$, shown visually as "do one transformation,
  then another." Exactly this lesson's non-commutativity trap, made intuitive.
  Watch after you've done the by-hand $AB$ computation.
- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — Inverse matrices, column space and null space (Essence of Linear Algebra, Ch. 7)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uQhTuRlWMxw)
  (video, ~12 min) — the "undo" idea behind $A^{-1}$, and a geometric picture of
  *why a singular matrix has no inverse* (it squashes space and can't be reversed).
  Watch after the lesson's identity/inverse section; ignore the "column space / null
  space" vocabulary for now — the inverse half is what you need.
