# Resources: Distance, similarity & the geometry of data

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — Dot products and duality (Essence of Linear Algebra, Ch. 9)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LyGKycYT2v0)
  (video) — the best visual for *projection as a shadow* and why the dot product
  measures "how much of one vector points along another." Watch after this lesson's
  "The formal version" projection section to see the shadow idea move.
- 🎮 [Setosa — Principal Component Analysis, visually explained](https://setosa.io/ev/principal-component-analysis/)
  (interactive) — drag points in 2-D and 3-D and watch a *summary direction* emerge; the
  17-dimensional UK-food example shows why one well-chosen direction can stand in for
  many columns. This is exactly the lesson's "seeing high-dimensional points in a
  low-dimensional picture" idea — treat it as intuition only, since the PCA *mechanics*
  are course-level and out of scope here.
- 📖 [KDnuggets — Distance Metrics: Euclidean, Manhattan, Minkowski, Oh My!](https://www.kdnuggets.com/2023/03/distance-metrics-euclidean-manhattan-minkowski-oh.html)
  (article) — a clean, free, beginner-friendly walk through Euclidean and Manhattan
  distance with small worked examples; good reinforcement of this lesson's
  square-add-root vs. add-the-absolute-gaps distinction. Read after the "Common traps"
  section.
