# Resources: Probability, odds, and log-odds

External explanations for this foundation module. All links verified.

- 📺 [StatQuest: Odds and Log(Odds), Clearly Explained!!!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ARfXDSkQf1Y)
  (video, Josh Starmer) — the perfect companion to this lesson: builds odds from counts and
  probabilities, then shows why the *log* of the odds is symmetric and well-behaved. Plain
  English, small numeric examples — exactly this learner's style. Watch after the toy
  example.
- 📺 [StatQuest: Odds Ratios and Log(Odds Ratios), Clearly Explained!!!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8nm0G-1uJzA)
  (video, Josh Starmer) — extends the idea to *ratios* of odds (the seed of practice
  Problem 5 here, and the way logistic-regression coefficients get read later). Watch it
  once you're comfortable with a single event's odds and want the comparison-of-two-groups
  view.
