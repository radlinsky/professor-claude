# Resources: Exponentials and logs

External explanations for this foundation module. All links verified.

- 📺 [3Blue1Brown: What's so special about Euler's number e?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=m2MIpDrF7Es)
  (video, Essence of Calculus ch. 5) — a beautiful visual account of what $e$ *is* and why
  it's the natural base for growth. Watch after the lesson's toy example, when you want the
  "why $e$ and not 10?" intuition deepened. Goes a little further than this module needs
  (into derivatives), but the first half is exactly on point.
- 📺 [Eddie Woo: Introduction to Logarithms (Rethinking Exponentials)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2V5h5gMMVGo)
  (video, ~11 min) — introduces the logarithm precisely as the *inverse* of an exponential
  — the "undo button" framing this lesson uses. A calm, blackboard-paced refresher; watch
  it if "$\log$ answers: $e$ to what power?" didn't fully click.
