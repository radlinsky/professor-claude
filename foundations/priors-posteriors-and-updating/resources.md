# Resources: Priors, posteriors & updating

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [StatQuest: Bayes' Theorem, Clearly Explained!!!!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9wCnvr7Xw4E)
  (video) — Josh Starmer walks the prior → likelihood → posterior update step by step on
  a small, concrete example, in the plain-English, tiny-numbers style this learner likes.
  Watch after "The formal version" to hear the posterior ∝ likelihood × prior story told
  a second way.
- 📺 [3Blue1Brown: Bayes' theorem, the geometry of changing beliefs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HZGCoVF3YvM)
  (video, ~15 min) — the single best animation of the "narrow the world, then take the
  fraction" idea, drawn as areas that shrink and rescale. Exactly this lesson's
  conditional-probability picture, made visual. Watch after "A tiny example first."
- 🎮 [Seeing Theory: Bayesian Inference](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/bayesian-inference/index.html)
  (interactive) — drag a prior and watch the posterior update in real time, with an
  interactive coin-flip + Beta-distribution demo that mirrors this lesson's continuous
  posterior over a bias. Play with it after "A distribution over a parameter" to feel the
  curve narrow as data grows.
