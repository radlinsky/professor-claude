# Resources: Integration and area under a curve

Verified external explanations for this foundation module. Watch/read alongside the
lesson if a second voice helps.

- 📺 **[3Blue1Brown — Integration and the fundamental theorem of calculus (Essence of Calculus, Ch. 8)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfG8ce4nNh0)**
  (video, ~20 min). Builds the integral visually as the area under a curve — exactly
  this lesson's "infinitely thin rectangles" idea — and derives the Fundamental
  Theorem from first principles with beautiful animation. The clearest explanation of
  why antiderivatives and area are the same thing. Watch this first if the
  "antiderivative gives area" connection hasn't clicked.

- 📺 **[3Blue1Brown — What does area have to do with slope? (Essence of Calculus, Ch. 9)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FnJqaIESC2s)**
  (video, ~12 min). The companion chapter: shows intuitively why differentiation and
  integration are inverse operations. Pairs perfectly with this lesson's Concept 2
  (antiderivatives and the Fundamental Theorem).

- 🎮 **[Khan Academy — Accumulation and Riemann sums](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/ap-calculus-ab/ab-integration-new/ab-6-2/v/simple-riemann-approximation-using-rectangles)**
  (interactive exercises). Step-by-step practice with left, right, and midpoint
  rectangle approximations — useful if you want more reps on the "area as rectangles"
  idea before the antiderivative rules.
