# Resources: LDA, QDA & naive Bayes

All links verified 2026-07-12.

- 📺 [StatQuest: Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA), Clearly Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=azXCzI57Yfc)
  (video) --- Josh Starmer walks through LDA's logic: model each class as a Gaussian,
  compute the discriminant score, classify. Watch after the lesson to see the same
  "plug into Bayes' theorem" idea animated with different visuals.
- 📖 [ISLR §4.4--4.5 — Generative Models for Classification](https://hastie.su.domains/ISLR2/ISLRv2_corrected_June_2023.pdf)
  (book, PDF) --- the textbook treatment this module follows: LDA (§4.4.1--4.4.4),
  QDA (§4.4.5), naive Bayes (§4.4.6), and the six-scenario comparison showing no
  single method dominates (§4.5). Read after the lesson for the formal derivations
  and the full simulation study.
- 📺 [StatQuest: Naive Bayes, Clearly Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O2L2Uv9pdDA)
  (video) --- a concise explanation of the conditional independence assumption and
  how naive Bayes estimates class-conditional densities one predictor at a time.
  Watch after the naive Bayes section of the lesson.
