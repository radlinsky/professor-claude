# Resources: The algebra moves a derivation is made of

All links verified 2026-07-04.

- 📺 [Professor Dave Explains — Multiplying Binomials by the FOIL Method](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RTC7RIwdZcE)
  (video) — a plain, step-by-step walk through expanding a product of two brackets (the
  distributive-property engine behind $(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$, including the cross
  term this lesson's Trap 1 warns about). Watch after Move 1 if the expand/factor step
  feels shaky — it drills exactly that mechanic.
- 📺 [3Blue1Brown — The simpler quadratic formula (Lockdown live math, Ep. 1)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MHXO86wKeDY)
  (video, long-form) — a visual, intuition-first take on *completing the square* and where
  it leads. Watch after this lesson's "completing the square (recognize it by name)"
  section if you want to see *why* forcing a $(x+k)^2$ to appear is such a useful move;
  you only need the completing-the-square portion, not the whole derivation.
